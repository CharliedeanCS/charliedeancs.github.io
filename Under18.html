What our Under 18 program is about

Our program provides students with the truly unique opportunity to not only experience the magic of one of the most captivating locations on the planet, but also the chance to make a profound, positive impact on the lives of young people aged 6-8 years old who fnd themselves in less fortunate circumstances than themselves.

Applicants for the Camp Thailand program will partake in a variety of riveting community work. For instance, in Thailand education isn’t government run; this has a detrimental effect on the prospects for young people, who often struggle to fulfl their optimum potential. Schools possess scarce learning resources; the program enables students to partake in a rewarding fundraising scheme and take essential equipment to their host school.

During their time at Camp, students will be provided with the chance to shape a brighter future for these young children, teaching English lessons, coaching sports, and getting involved in the exciting Temple School project where we are building a renovating a new school for the local community.

Whilst in Thailand, students will also be presented with an incredible opportunity to visit an elephant conversation, during which time they will clean and care for elephants, the much-respected and national symbol of Thailand community.

These activities are representative of a once-in-a-lifetime venture, a trip whereby students can broaden their horizons, enhance their self-confdence, gain additional responsibility and proft from valued skills for their CV that can be transferred within the world of work upon returning to the UK. In preparation for their trip, each participant will be enrolled onto a 30-hour online teaching Young Learners course, during which time they will be trained for their time in the classroom, More information about this on the Teaching Qualifcations page

Camp Thailand has sent over 3000 young people on the program since it launched 3 years ago and are set to launch an over 50’s program in January.
